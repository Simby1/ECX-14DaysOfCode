Dos and Don'ts for Cat Owners:

Dos

  • Provide a safe and stimulating environment: This includes scratching posts, climbing trees, and plenty of toys to keep them entertained.
  • Offer a balanced and nutritious diet: Consult your vet for the best food choices for your cat's age and health.
  • Ensure access to fresh water at all times: Multiple water bowls or a pet water fountain can encourage hydration.
  • Schedule regular veterinary check-ups: Preventative care is crucial for your cat's long-term health.

Don'ts

  • Never declaw your cat: This is a painful and unnecessary surgical procedure.
  • Don't use physical punishment: This can damage your bond and make your cat fearful.
  • Don't force interactions: Let your cat come to you on their own terms.
  • Don't ignore changes in behavior or appetite: These can be signs of underlying health issues.

Cat Breeds for Adoption

While specific cats available for adoption will vary, here are some common and popular cat breeds you can find at our adoption center, along with a brief note on their general temperament. Click the images to see more of each Breed!

Domestic Shorthair/Longhair (Mixed Breed)

These are the most common cats in shelters and rescues. They come in an incredible variety of colors, patterns, and personalities, making them wonderfully unique companions. Often healthy and adaptable.

Siamese

Known for their striking blue almond-shaped eyes, vocal nature, and affectionate personalities. They tend to be very interactive and can be quite demanding of attention.

Ragdoll

Large, docile, and affectionate cats that often go limp when held (hence the name). They are known for their calm demeanor and striking blue eyes.
